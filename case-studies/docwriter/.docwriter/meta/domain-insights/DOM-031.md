## Insight

The Xperience access check chain has 5 layers: content-role binding → member-role binding → auth cookie claims → SecurityStampValidator revalidation → IsInRole check. Documentation must trace the full chain to explain temporal behavior (e.g., why role removal doesn't immediately revoke access). 33% of tasks (6/18) in DOC-3167 needed to reference portions of this chain, making it the most cross-referenced behavioral finding in the run.

## Evidence

- DOC-3167: FIND-047 is the cross-cutting finding documenting the full access check chain.
- Tasks touching the chain: T-001 (session behavior), T-002 (cookie revalidation stale-fix), T-006 (content item security), T-007 (page security), T-010 (content retriever API), T-012 (forms authentication).
- 6/18 tasks (33%) touched the access check chain — the highest cross-reference count for any behavioral finding.
- Related findings: FIND-028, FIND-029, FIND-030.

## Applicability

Applicable to any documentation task involving Xperience content security, authentication, or session management. When any layer of the chain changes, all pages referencing any other layer may need updates to maintain consistency in temporal behavior explanations.
