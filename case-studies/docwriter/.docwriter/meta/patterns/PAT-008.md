## Insight

Injecting both patterns AND anti-patterns from the meta-knowledge brief into per-task invariant briefings measurably improves first-attempt acceptance. Tasks that explicitly cited anti-patterns in their invariantsApplied achieved first-attempt success, while tasks that did not cite anti-patterns fell to the very failures the anti-patterns warned about.

## Evidence

- T-009 explicitly cited ANTI-002 (em dash avoidance) and achieved first-attempt success; T-010/T-011/T-016 did not cite it and all had em dash rejections
- T-006 cited 3 meta-knowledge patterns (PAT-001, PAT-002, PAT-004) and achieved first-attempt success on the highest-complexity task
- Signal strength: medium (3+ tasks show the contrast pattern within a single run)
- Confidence: low (first observation in a single run)

## Recommendation

Content writer per-task briefings should include relevant anti-patterns (not just positive patterns) as explicit items to avoid. Anti-pattern awareness is as valuable as positive pattern guidance.

## Applicability

Applicable to any pipeline run using the meta-knowledge brief. Most valuable when anti-patterns have concrete, mechanical mitigations (e.g., grep for em dash).
