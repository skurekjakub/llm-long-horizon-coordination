## Key Structural Decisions

### 1. Chapter 16 as Maximum Black Moment
Positioned contract revelation at highest false security (after Ch15 "I want you" confession) to create maximum betrayal impact. Every beat in Ch12-15 builds trust that Ch16 obliterates.

**Design rationale:** Black moment works structurally only if the preceding hope feels real. Ch15 provides genuine intimacy and imagined future, making the betrayal revelation in Ch16 devastating rather than predictable.

### 2. Contract Loophole (Ch20) as Dual-Arc Convergence
Temporary death/resurrection dramatizes TP1 (trust as vulnerability to destruction) and TP3 (chosen love after necessity removed). Fantasy solution (magic loophole) requires romance proof (ultimate trust).

**Design rationale:** The most satisfying dual-genre climaxes occur when both arcs require each other. Kaelith's consent to death is the romance proof; Lirien's precision execution is the fantasy solution. Neither works without the other.

### 3. Scene-Sequel NOT Formally Enforced
Craft profile explicitly excludes T1/T2/T3/T14 due to emotionally restrained prose style. Beat design focuses on micro-tension, subtext, and voice-specific observation rather than formulaic scene structure.

**Design rationale:** The restrained style ("reveals through omission and gesture, not declaration") means sequel beats are silences, deflections, and physical actions rather than emotion→thought→decision sequences. Formal enforcement would push prose toward explicit interiority incompatible with the tone contract.

### 4. Thematic Weight Per Beat
T25 (Thematic Argument Scaffolding) is active. Each enriched chapter maps how specific beats advance the three thematic pillars, ensuring themes are dramatized through action rather than stated.

**Design rationale:** Without explicit beat-level thematic assignment, themes become abstract intentions rather than embodied story. Tracking which beats advance TP1/TP2/TP3 ensures the thematic argument is built through character choices, not narration.
