# Chapter Twenty-Two: What the Water Chose

The first thing she classified was the light.

Not the quality she had trained her eyes to assess in a thousand operational mornings — light that told you the hour, the weather, the number of exits worth attempting. This was something else. The light coming through the narrow window of the shelter at the base of Serath Dûn was grey and gold simultaneously, the Pallor’s silver filter thinned to something gossamer, and through it, for the first time in Lirien’s memory, the dawn arrived carrying actual warmth — heat that reached her directly, the sun’s intention unmediated by the divine remains.
